Master the Art of Authentic Neapolitan Pizza Making

Indulge in the rich flavors and traditions of Naples with our comprehensive guide to crafting the perfect Neapolitan pizza. Uncover the secrets behind the dough, sauce, and toppings that define this iconic Italian dish. Our step-by-step tutorial provides the expertise you need to recreate this culinary masterpiece in your own kitchen. Discover the essence of Neapolitan pizza-making and elevate your homemade pizza game to a whole new level of deliciousness.

  • The dough

  • - 700g tipo 00 flour
  • - 500g water
  • - 5g honey
  • - 5g fresh yeast
  • - 20g salt

  • Tomato sauce

  • - 500g canned San Marzano tomatoes
  • - 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
  • - Salt to taste
  • - Fresh basil leaves

  • Poolish (Starter)

  • - 200g tipo 00 flour
  • - 200g water
  • - 5g honey
    • li>- 5g fresh yeast
  • 1. Place the poolish in a closed container and refrigerate it for 24 hours to allow it to rise.
  1. 2. After 24 hours, remove the poolish from the fridge. Add the remaining flour, water, honey, fresh yeast, and salt to the poolish.
  3. 3. Mix all ingredients thoroughly, employing the slap-and-fold technique to aerate the dough. Ensure the dough is dense, yet pliable.
  5. 4. Let the dough rest for 15 minutes, drizzled with a little olive oil.
  6. 5. After 15 minutes, fold the dough again and cover it with a damp towel. Let it rest on the countertop for 1 hour.
  8. 6. Divide the dough into pizza balls of approximately 250-280g each and place them in a sealed container for another hour.
  9. 7. Spread a pile of flour (around 2dl) on the countertop. Take a pizza ball and place it upside down into the flour.
  10. 8. Gently press from the center outwards with flat fingers to push air into the corners. Flip it over and repeat, maintaining the original top as the upper surface. Lift and stretch as the video shows
  12. 9. Preheat your oven to its highest temperature, preferably using a pizza stone or baking steel.
  14. 10. Spread tomato sauce on the stretched dough, followed by fior di latte mozzarella cheese and a drizzle of olive oil.
  16. 11. Stretch the pizza to the desired size and carefully transfer it onto a wooden pizza peel to place it in the oven.
  17. 12. A quick motion is crucial to maintain the shape and size of the pizza.
  19. 13. In an authentic oven, cook the pizza for about 90 seconds, turning it halfway to ensure even cooking. In a regular household oven, give it some more time to finish cooking by using the highest heat setting.
  21. 14. The pizza should exhibit charred dots on the bottom, crispy edges, and a soft, slightly squishy interior.
  23. 15. Remove the pizza from the oven, apply some topping, slice it, and serve it fresh, savoring the authentic taste of Neapolitan pizza!
